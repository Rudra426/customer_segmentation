WHAT IS KAVAACH?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Kavaach is a weekly parametric income insurance product built
specifically for pharma delivery riders in India. The word
"parametric" means payouts are triggered automatically when
a measurable condition is met — no claim forms, no paperwork,
no waiting. If it rains heavily in your zone, your money
arrives on your phone within minutes.

Target user: A pharma delivery rider earning ₹4,000–₹7,000/week,
working for platforms like Tata 1mg, Apollo 24|7, PharmEasy,
or Netmeds — who loses income during weather disruptions,
platform outages, or zone shutdowns.

Weekly premium: ₹31–₹121/week (varies by risk profile)
Max weekly payout: ₹350–₹600
Payout method: UPI (instant, within minutes of trigger)


━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
WHY PHARMA SPECIFICALLY?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Every other team will build for food delivery. Pharma has
unique risks that food delivery does not:

1. COLD CHAIN: 30-40% of pharma orders are temperature-sensitive
   (insulin, vaccines). When ambient temperature crosses 42°C,
   platforms halt cold-chain deliveries entirely. Rider earns
   nothing — this income loss type doesn't exist in food delivery.

2. PATIENT DEPENDENCY: Patients ordering medicines are not
   choosing a luxury — they need medicine to survive. This
   creates guilt pressure on riders to deliver even in unsafe
   conditions. Insurance removes that pressure.

3. CONCENTRATED GEOGRAPHY: 90% of pickups happen near hospital
   clusters. One blocked road = all deliveries fail. Food
   delivery is spread across the city.

4. HIGHER PER-ORDER VALUE: ₹120–₹200 per delivery vs ₹50 for
   food. Every missed delivery hurts more.

THE 5 TRIGGERS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

These are the 5 conditions that automatically fire a payout:

Trigger 1 — MONSOON HALT
  Condition: Rainfall > threshold mm/hr for minimum duration
  API: OpenWeatherMap (free tier available)

Trigger 2 — HEAT CHAIN BREAK
  Condition: Temperature > threshold + rider handles cold-chain
  API: OpenWeatherMap

Trigger 3 — TOXIC AIR LOCKDOWN
  Condition: AQI > threshold for minimum duration
  API: OpenAQ (free, public)

Trigger 4 — PLATFORM BLACKOUT
  Condition: Zero orders for extended period during active shift
  API: Mock platform API (built by us for demo)

Trigger 5 — ZONE SHUTDOWN
  Condition: Active curfew or emergency alert in rider's zone
  API: Mock zone alert API (built by us for demo)

IMPORTANT: Thresholds are NOT the same for every rider.
They depend on delivery model (explained below).

THREE DELIVERY MODELS — THE KEY INSIGHT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Pharma delivery is NOT one uniform job. There are 3 models:

HYPERLOCAL (1–2 hour delivery)
  Platforms: Apollo 24|7, Medstown, PhonePe Pincode
  Cities: Metros only
  Risk: HIGHEST — zero buffer, every hour counts
  Payout formula: Hourly rate × disruption hours

SAME-DAY (4–8 hour delivery)
  Platforms: Tata 1mg, PharmEasy standard
  Cities: Tier 1 + large Tier 2
  Risk: MEDIUM — can recover from short disruptions
  Payout formula: Daily rate × payout percentage

SCHEDULED (next day / 1–3 days)
  Platforms: Netmeds, regional pharma apps
  Cities: Tier 2, Tier 3, pan-India
  Risk: LOWEST — only prolonged events cause real loss
  Payout formula: Daily rate × disruption days

Example — same 3-hour rain event:
  Hyperlocal rider loses: ₹900 (15% of weekly income)
  Same-day rider loses:   ₹240 (4% of weekly income)
  Scheduled rider loses:  ₹0   (reschedules easily)

So trigger thresholds, payout calculations, and premiums
are ALL different per delivery model.

HOW WE DETECT DELIVERY MODEL (NO USER INPUT NEEDED)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

We ask riders: which platform do you work for?
We already know their pincode from onboarding.

Platform + Pincode → we look up our coverage map →
delivery model is auto-assigned. Rider just confirms.

For hackathon: hardcoded JSON map of top 100 pincodes × 
5 platforms = covers 80% of real use cases.

For production: Tata 1mg has an official partner API
(onedoc.1mg.com) that returns serviceability + ETA live.
For other platforms: weekly web scraper that checks their
website and refreshes our map.

When a platform expands to a new city (like 1mg did in
Jaipur recently), our scraper detects the change, upgrades
the rider's coverage tier, and adjusts their premium
automatically — no reinstallation needed.

Multi-platform riders (work for 1mg + PharmEasy both):
→ We apply the HIGHER risk model to ensure full protection.

HOW WE VERIFY RIDER'S INCOME (FRAUD PREVENTION)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

We don't just trust what riders declare. Three methods:

1. SMS PARSING (hackathon build — 1-2 days)
   Ask permission to read SMS inbox (Android).
   Parse UPI credit messages from "1mg Pay", "PharmEasy",
   "Apollo Payments" → calculate real weekly average.
   This directly shows normal weeks vs disruption weeks.

2. ACCOUNT AGGREGATOR FRAMEWORK (mention in pitch)
   India's RBI-regulated AA system lets us request verified
   bank transaction data with rider's explicit consent.
   No raw credentials shared — fully legal under DPDP Act.
   Use Setu AA API (free sandbox). Eliminates income fraud.

3. STATISTICAL CAPS + ML FLAGGING
   Max declarable income: ₹15,000/week (99th percentile).
   ML Isolation Forest model flags claims where declared
   income is far above zone average → admin review.

Fraud detection uses: GPS zone mismatch, weather API
confirmation, time since policy start, claim frequency,
duplicate detection — all as model features.

TECH STACK (SHORT)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Frontend  : React + Vite (PWA — installable on Android)
Backend   : Node.js + Express + Socket.io
ML        : Python + FastAPI (separate microservice)
Database  : MongoDB Atlas (free tier)
Cache     : Redis / Upstash (trigger deduplication)
Queue     : Bull Queue (trigger engine, runs every 15 min)
Payments  : Razorpay test mode (UPI)
Blockchain: Solidity on Ethereum Sepolia (audit trail)
SMS/OTP   : MSG91 or Twilio trial
Weather   : OpenWeatherMap API (free tier)
AQI       : OpenAQ API (free, public)

Mobile-first by default — all UI designed for a 6-inch
Android screen first, desktop second. Hindi language
option built with react-i18next.

WHAT WE STILL HAVE TO FIGURE OUT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

These are open questions that need team discussion or more
research before building:

1. PLATFORM BLACKOUT DETECTION
   How do we ACTUALLY know if a platform has zero orders?
   We don't have real API access to 1mg/Apollo order feeds.
   Current plan: mock API for demo, but real implementation
   needs either a B2B deal with the platform OR we ask riders
   to share their app screen data with consent.
   → NEEDS DECISION: Keep as mock, or build a rider-reported
     flow ("tap here if your app shows no orders")?

2. LEGAL / IRDAI COMPLIANCE
   Parametric insurance in India requires either:
   a) IRDAI sandbox licence (for startups — takes months)
   b) Partnership with a licensed insurer as the underwriter
   For the hackathon this is fine to skip. But if this goes
   to production, we need a licensed insurance partner.
   → NEEDS RESEARCH: Which Indian insurers have sandbox APIs?
     (ICICI Lombard, Bajaj Allianz both do B2B parametric)

3. COLD CHAIN VERIFICATION
   We flag riders as "handles cold chain" based on self-declaration.
   Platforms don't expose an API saying "this rider is
   cold-chain certified." So the flag could be wrong.
   → NEEDS DECISION: Accept self-declaration with a fraud cap,
     OR build an upload flow for platform certificate?

4. REAL UPI PAYOUT
   Razorpay test mode works for demo. Real UPI payouts to
   riders (not just to platforms) require a different product:
   Razorpay Route / RazorpayX Payout API. These need KYC
   of the business entity (not just test credentials).
   → NEEDS DECISION: Is RazorpayX free sandbox available for
     hackathon? Check their developer docs.

5. PINCODE MAP COMPLETENESS
   Our static coverage map will cover ~100 pincodes for demo.
   Edge cases: rider in a pincode not in our map, new city,
   platform just launched there.
   → NEEDS DECISION: What is the DEFAULT model when we don't
     know? (Safe choice: same_day — middle ground)

6. MULTI-PLATFORM RIDER PREMIUM
   If a rider works for both 1mg (hyperlocal) and PharmEasy
   (same-day), we cover the higher risk model and charge
   the higher premium. But is that fair if only 20% of their
   income comes from the hyperlocal platform?
   → NEEDS DECISION: Weighted premium by platform income
     split? Or always highest model? Simpler = always highest.

7. BLOCKCHAIN GAS FEES
   Every payout creates an Ethereum transaction. On Sepolia
   testnet this is free. On mainnet this costs real ETH.
   For production: either use a Layer 2 (Polygon, Arbitrum)
   or abstract gas fees via a relayer. Testnet is fine for demo.
   → DECISION FOR LATER: Polygon PoS is the cheapest option.

8. HINDI LANGUAGE SCOPE
   We said Hindi-first. But how much of the app exactly?
   All screens? Just rider-facing? Notifications too?
   → NEEDS DECISION: For hackathon, at minimum the premium
     screen and payout notification should be in Hindi.
     Full Hindi pass = Phase 3.
