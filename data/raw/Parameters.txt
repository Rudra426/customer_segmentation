CATEGORY A: EARNINGS PARAMETERS (Primary Premium Drivers)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. weekly_earnings (₹1,500 - ₹15,000)
   Source: Rider onboarding + SMS parsing
   Impact: 35-40% of premium variation
   Why: Higher earner = more income at risk = higher premium

2. deliveries_per_day (1 - 30)
   Source: Rider declares at onboarding
   Impact: 5-8% of premium variation
   Why: More deliveries = more exposure to disruptions

3. is_part_time (0 or 1)
   Source: Auto-calculated (< 5 deliveries/day = part-time)
   Impact: 3-5% of premium variation
   Why: Part-timers lose less absolute income per disruption

4. earnings_consistency (0.0 - 1.0)
   Source: Rolling 4-week variance calculation
   Impact: 2-4% of premium variation
   Why: Consistent earner = predictable risk = slight discount
   Lower variance = higher score = lower premium

CATEGORY B: LOCATION RISK PARAMETERS
────────────────────────────────────────
5. flood_risk (0.0 - 1.0)
   Source: Historical flood data by pincode
   Impact: 12-15% of premium variation
   Why: High flood zones trigger monsoon payouts more often
   Examples: Mumbai (0.85), Jaisalmer (0.05)

6. heat_risk (0.0 - 1.0)
   Source: IMD historical temperature data by city
   Impact: 8-12% of premium variation
   Why: Extreme heat triggers cold-chain halt payouts
   Examples: Jaisalmer (0.95), Bengaluru (0.30)

7. aqi_risk (0.0 - 1.0)
   Source: CPCB historical AQI data by city
   Impact: 6-10% of premium variation
   Why: High AQI zones trigger toxic air lockdown payouts
   Examples: Delhi (0.95), Coimbatore (0.30)

8. cyclone_risk (0.0 - 1.0)
   Source: IMD cyclone track historical data
   Impact: 4-8% of premium variation
   Why: Coastal zones face cyclone-related shutdowns
   Examples: Bhubaneswar (0.75), Jaipur (0.02)

9. coastal_zone (0 or 1)
   Source: Geographic lookup by city
   Impact: 2-5% of premium variation
   Why: Coastal = additional cyclone + storm surge risk

10. city_tier (1, 2, or 3)
    Source: Standard city classification
    Impact: 3-6% of premium variation
    Why: Tier 1 = better infrastructure but higher disruption impact

11. zone_type (hospital_adjacent / residential / commercial / industrial)
    Source: Rider selects at onboarding
    Impact: 4-7% of premium variation
    Why: Hospital zones = concentrated delivery risk (one road block = all orders fail)

CATEGORY C: DELIVERY MODEL PARAMETERS
─────────────────────────────────────────
12. delivery_model (hyperlocal / same_day / scheduled)
    Source: Auto-detected from platform + pincode
    Impact: 18-22% of premium variation (second highest!)
    Why: Hyperlocal = zero buffer = highest risk
         Scheduled = multi-day window = lowest risk
    Multipliers:
      hyperlocal → 1.40×
      same_day   → 1.00× (baseline)
      scheduled  → 0.70×

13. platform (Apollo24x7 / Tata1mg / PharmEasy / Netmeds / PhonePePincode)
    Source: Rider selects at onboarding
    Impact: 3-6% of premium variation
    Why: Platform reliability varies (PhonePe has fewer outages than others)

CATEGORY D: RIDER PROFILE PARAMETERS
────────────────────────────────────────
14. cold_chain (0 or 1)
    Source: Rider declares at onboarding
    Impact: 8-12% of premium variation
    Why: Cold-chain handlers face heat-triggered halts (Trigger 2)
    Premium increase: +15% if cold_chain = 1

15. experience_months (1 - 84)
    Source: Rider declares at onboarding
    Impact: 2-4% of premium variation
    Why: Experienced riders navigate disruptions better
    Discount: Up to 10% for 5+ years experience

16. multi_platform (0 or 1)
    Source: Rider declares if they work for 2+ platforms
    Impact: 2-5% of premium variation
    Why: Multi-platform = income diversification = lower single-platform risk

CATEGORY E: TEMPORAL PARAMETERS
───────────────────────────────────
17. seasonal_index (0.0 - 1.0)
    Source: Auto-calculated from current month
    Impact: 10-15% of premium variation
    Why: Monsoon months (June-Sept) = highest risk
    Calculation:
      June-Sept  → 0.70 - 1.00 (peak monsoon)
      Apr-May    → 0.45 - 0.75 (summer + pre-monsoon)
      Oct-Mar    → 0.15 - 0.45 (winter + dry season)

18. month (1 - 12)
    Source: System timestamp
    Impact: 3-5% of premium variation
    Why: Complements seasonal_index for fine-tuning

CATEGORY F: LOYALTY / HISTORY PARAMETERS
────────────────────────────────────────────
19. no_claim_weeks (0 - 4+)
    Source: Your database (tracks weeks without claims)
    Impact: 3-6% of premium variation
    Why: Clean history = lower fraud risk = discount

20. no_claim_discount (0.0 - 0.15)
    Source: Auto-calculated (no_claim_weeks × 0.03)
    Impact: Direct multiplier on final premium
    Why: Rewards riders who don't claim unnecessarily
    Max discount: 15% after 5+ clean weeks
